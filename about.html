Midterm Project for CIS 233DA Spring 2020

The project assignment was to create an instructional guide based on a topic of our choosing. In addition to the topic lessons, the website must be responsive and provide references to resources used throughout the research process.

I chose the topic of CSS Grid because I have had little experience with it and wanted to better understand the benefits of using it.

From my perspective, CSS Grid is a quick way to layout your page areas. If you know exactly how you want your areas to be positioned and their sizes, you can explicitly define those areas and position them on the grid. It's also useful for experimenting with layouts and laying out areas in a way that would've been extremely difficult, like laying areas on top of other areas and adjusting their z-index.

In conjunction with media queries, you can adjust the grid for different screen sizes and easily control the layout for smaller screen and mobile views. When it comes to grid items, the implicit grid is amazing. Especially when creating an image gallery and not knowing exactly how many images your gallery will contain. You can define your grid columns, grid rows, but also define how new grid items will be placed outside of your defined grid.

To make CSS Grid really useful I believe an understanding of Flexbox is essential. Espeically when it comes to controlling specific grid items or spacing them.
